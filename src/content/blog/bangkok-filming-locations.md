The best Bangkok filming locations for corporate videos are the Chao Phraya River corridor, luxury hotel properties like the Mandarin Oriental, modern co-working spaces such as True Digital Park, and cultural landmarks including Wat Arun and Yaowarat (Chinatown). These locations offer a mix of modern skyline backdrops, traditional Thai aesthetics, and versatile interior spaces that suit everything from brand films and executive interviews to product launches and event coverage.

Bangkok is one of Southeast Asia's most visually rich cities for video production. The contrast between gleaming skyscrapers and centuries-old temples, between chaotic street markets and serene riverside terraces, gives production teams an extraordinary range of backdrops within a single city. Whether you are producing a company profile, a recruitment video, a product demo, or a social media campaign, the right location sets the tone for your entire project.

This guide covers 12 proven Bangkok filming locations that our production team uses regularly, with practical details on permits, timing, and the type of corporate video each location serves best.

## 1. Chao Phraya River and Riverside Promenades

**Best for:** Brand films, company profiles, cinematic B-roll, [drone and aerial video](/services/drone-aerial-video/)

The Chao Phraya River is Bangkok's most iconic natural feature, and the riverside promenades on both the Thonburi and Bangkok sides offer sweeping views of the city skyline, historic bridges, and traditional longboat traffic. The ASIATIQUE pier area, the Tha Maharaj waterfront, and the areas near Wat Arun all provide distinct visual palettes.

**Permit requirements:** Filming along public riverside walkways requires a permit from the Bangkok Metropolitan Administration (BMA). Private piers and hotel docks handle their own permissions. Drone permits require CAAT approval and coordination with local authorities due to proximity to Wat Arun and the Grand Palace restricted zones.

**Best time to film:** Golden hour (5:30-6:30 PM) delivers the most cinematic light as the sun sets behind the temples on the Thonburi side. Early mornings (5:30-7:00 AM) offer calm water and minimal boat traffic for clean reflection shots.

**Insider tip:** Book a private longtail boat as a mobile filming platform. This costs around 1,500-2,500 THB per hour and lets you capture tracking shots along the river without needing a fixed location permit. The boat captains who operate from Tha Tien pier are experienced with film crews.

## 2. Rooftop Bars and Sky Decks

**Best for:** Executive interviews, brand films, product launches, social media content

Bangkok's rooftop scene is world-famous, and locations like Vertigo at Banyan Tree, Sky Bar at Lebua, Octave at Marriott Sukhumvit, and Mahanakhon SkyWalk offer dramatic skyline backdrops. These spaces communicate ambition, success, and a global perspective, which makes them ideal for leadership interviews and premium brand content.

**Permit requirements:** All filming is arranged directly with the hotel or venue management. Expect to pay a location fee (typically 15,000-50,000 THB for a half-day) and potentially a food-and-beverage minimum. Most venues require a production brief and proof of insurance.

**Best time to film:** Arrive at 4:00 PM for daylight skyline shots, then film through blue hour (6:15-6:45 PM) for the most dramatic backdrop. Weekday afternoons before 5:00 PM offer the emptiest rooftops.

**Insider tip:** Negotiate access during closed hours. Most rooftop venues open at 5:00 or 6:00 PM. If you can secure a 2:00-5:00 PM window, you get full control of the space without paying for guest displacement. Wind is a constant challenge at height, so bring sandbags for light stands and plan for external audio challenges.

## 3. True Digital Park

**Best for:** Tech company profiles, startup culture videos, innovation-themed content, recruitment videos

True Digital Park in the Punnawithi area is Southeast Asia's largest tech and startup hub. The open-plan co-working floors, meeting pods, event spaces, and digital art installations create a modern, innovation-forward visual environment. The architecture is clean and contemporary with plenty of natural light.

**Permit requirements:** Contact the True Digital Park events and partnerships team directly. They have an established process for film crews and typically require 5-7 days advance booking. Rates are competitive compared to traditional studio rental.

**Best time to film:** Weekday mornings between 9:00 and 11:00 AM when the space is active enough to feel energetic but not so crowded that you lose control of your background. Avoid lunch hours (12:00-1:30 PM) when foot traffic peaks.

**Insider tip:** The outdoor terrace areas and the connecting walkways to Punnawithi BTS station offer excellent transitional shots. The digital signage screens throughout the building can sometimes be customized for your brand if you coordinate in advance.

## 4. The Great Room

**Best for:** Professional interviews, client testimonials, business-focused brand films

The Great Room is a premium co-working space with locations in Gaysorn Tower and other central Bangkok addresses. The interiors feature warm wood tones, designer furniture, and curated art, creating a polished corporate atmosphere that reads well on camera without appearing sterile.

**Permit requirements:** Arrange directly with The Great Room management. They are generally receptive to production inquiries, particularly during off-peak hours. A small crew (3-5 people) can often be accommodated with minimal disruption to members.

**Best time to film:** Early morning (7:30-9:00 AM) before the space fills up, or mid-afternoon (2:00-4:00 PM) when many members are in meetings and the main floor is quieter.

**Insider tip:** The Gaysorn Tower location has floor-to-ceiling windows with excellent natural light and a Sukhumvit skyline view. Position interview subjects near these windows for a naturally lit setup that requires minimal additional lighting gear.

## 5. Mandarin Oriental Bangkok

**Best for:** Luxury brand content, high-end corporate profiles, hospitality marketing, [event videography](/services/event-videography/)

The Mandarin Oriental is Bangkok's most storied luxury hotel, with a heritage dating to 1876. The Authors' Lounge, the riverside terrace, the landscaped gardens, and the colonial-era architecture provide an atmosphere of timeless elegance. This location signals prestige and is ideal for brands that want to associate with old-world sophistication.

**Permit requirements:** All filming requires approval from the hotel's public relations and marketing department. Expect a formal proposal process, location fee negotiation (budget 30,000-100,000+ THB depending on scope), and restrictions on which areas can be filmed. Lead time of 2-4 weeks is typical.

**Best time to film:** Mid-morning (9:00-11:00 AM) when the riverside terrace has soft light and minimal guest traffic. The Authors' Lounge photographs best in the late morning when light fills the room through the east-facing windows.

**Insider tip:** The hotel's river shuttle boats and the private pier area are often overlooked as filming locations. These provide a unique perspective and a branded transport experience that adds production value to hospitality or luxury lifestyle content.

## 6. Rosewood Bangkok

**Best for:** Modern luxury brand content, fashion and lifestyle videos, corporate event coverage

The Rosewood Bangkok in the Phloen Chit area represents contemporary Thai luxury. The lobby, the Nan Bei restaurant, and the upper-floor suites offer a design-forward aesthetic with clean lines, rich materials, and striking art installations. Where the Mandarin Oriental conveys heritage, the Rosewood conveys modern sophistication.

**Permit requirements:** Similar to the Mandarin Oriental, contact the PR and marketing team with a detailed production brief. Location fees and access restrictions apply. The hotel is generally more flexible with contemporary and lifestyle-oriented productions.

**Best time to film:** The lobby and ground-floor spaces receive beautiful filtered light in the early afternoon. Upper-floor suites with city views are best filmed during golden hour for a warm, editorial quality.

**Insider tip:** The vertical garden in the main building and the rooftop pool area are two of the most visually distinctive features. If your production brief can include these spaces, they add significant visual impact that sets your content apart from generic hotel footage.

## 7. Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn)

**Best for:** Cultural brand storytelling, tourism content, establishing shots, cinematic B-roll, [drone and aerial video](/services/drone-aerial-video/)

Wat Arun is arguably Bangkok's most recognizable landmark. The towering prang covered in colorful porcelain, the riverside setting, and the surrounding temple grounds offer an unmistakable sense of place. For corporate videos that need to establish a Bangkok or Thailand context immediately, Wat Arun delivers that in a single frame.

**Permit requirements:** Filming at Wat Arun requires a permit from the temple administration and, for larger productions, coordination with the Fine Arts Department. Permit fees range from 2,000 to 20,000 THB depending on crew size and equipment. Tripods and professional cameras always require a permit. Strict dress codes apply for all crew members.

**Best time to film:** Sunrise (5:45-6:30 AM) from the Bangkok side of the river captures the temple bathed in warm light with the river in the foreground. Late afternoon from the Thonburi side works well for silhouette and backlit compositions. Avoid the 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM tourist peak entirely.

**Insider tip:** The small courtyard behind the main prang is rarely visited by tourists and offers intimate framing options. For the classic postcard shot, position your camera at Tha Tien pier or the rooftop of Sala Rattanakosin hotel across the river.

## 8. Lumpini Park and Benjakitti Park

**Best for:** Health and wellness brand content, outdoor corporate team videos, lifestyle B-roll, morning executive portraits

Lumpini Park is Bangkok's answer to Central Park: a 140-acre green oasis in the heart of the business district. Benjakitti Park, nearby, has been recently expanded with a stunning elevated walkway, wetland areas, and modern landscaping. Both parks offer a natural counterpoint to Bangkok's concrete jungle and work well for brands in wellness, fitness, sustainability, and lifestyle sectors.

**Permit requirements:** Both parks are managed by the BMA. Commercial filming permits are required and must be obtained in advance from the park administration office. Fees are modest (1,000-5,000 THB) but the approval process can take 5-10 business days. No filming is allowed during official park events.

**Best time to film:** Very early morning (5:30-7:00 AM) when joggers and tai chi practitioners create authentic background activity and the light is soft and directional. The Benjakitti elevated walkway is best photographed during golden hour when the surrounding skyscrapers reflect warm light.

**Insider tip:** Lumpini Park's palm-lined main avenue offers a natural leading line that works beautifully for walking-and-talking interview formats. Benjakitti's Queen Sirikit National Convention Center end has a skyline view that places your subject against modern Bangkok without leaving the park.

## 9. Chinatown (Yaowarat Road)

**Best for:** Street-level brand storytelling, food and hospitality content, dynamic B-roll, energetic social media content

Yaowarat Road and the surrounding Chinatown district are a feast of neon signs, gold shops, street food stalls, and narrow soi (alleyways) packed with visual texture. This location injects energy and authenticity into any production and works especially well for brands that want to communicate dynamism, street credibility, or cultural immersion.

**Permit requirements:** Street filming in Chinatown technically requires a BMA permit, but small crews with handheld equipment can often work discreetly. For larger setups with tripods, lights, and blocking, coordinate with the local district office (Samphanthawong District) at least one week in advance. Some shop owners will allow filming in or from their premises for a small fee.

**Best time to film:** The neon signs come alive at dusk (6:00-8:00 PM), which is the signature Yaowarat look. For less chaotic footage, arrive at 4:00-5:00 PM before the night market crowd builds. Sunday mornings are the quietest time for controlled shooting.

**Insider tip:** Soi Nana (not to be confused with the Sukhumvit Nana area) is a narrow alley off Yaowarat that has become a hub for creative cafes and bars inside converted shophouses. It offers a more controlled environment with Chinatown character, which is useful when you need the aesthetic without the crowd management challenges.

## 10. Talad Noi

**Best for:** Creative brand content, hipster and boutique brand videos, art-directed shoots, editorial-style corporate content

Talad Noi is a small neighborhood between Chinatown and the river that has become Bangkok's most photogenic "hidden" district. Crumbling Sino-Portuguese shophouses, street art murals, vine-covered facades, and repurposed warehouse cafes create an atmospheric backdrop that feels curated without being artificial. It communicates creativity, authenticity, and a connection to Bangkok's layered history.

**Permit requirements:** Most of Talad Noi is public streetscape, so small crews can film with handheld equipment without formal permits. For larger setups, coordinate with the local community leaders, who are generally supportive of respectful productions. Cafe and gallery owners in the area are accustomed to hosting shoots.

**Best time to film:** Morning light (7:00-9:00 AM) cuts through the narrow streets and creates dramatic shadow play on the textured walls. Weekday mornings are ideal as the neighborhood is quiet and you have more control over your compositions.

**Insider tip:** The abandoned warehouse spaces near the river end of Talad Noi can sometimes be rented for art-directed setups. The So Heng Tai Mansion, a 200-year-old Chinese manor house, is open for filming by appointment and offers an extraordinary interior unlike anything else in Bangkok.

## 11. ICONSIAM

**Best for:** Retail and consumer brand content, product launches, technology showcases, modern Bangkok establishing shots

ICONSIAM is Bangkok's most ambitious retail and lifestyle complex, situated on the Chao Phraya River in the Charoen Nakhon area. The dramatic architecture, the indoor floating market (SookSiam), the multimedia fountain on the river terrace, and the luxury retail floors provide a range of modern, high-production-value backdrops.

**Permit requirements:** All filming inside ICONSIAM requires approval from the mall's marketing department. Expect a formal application process, insurance requirements, and location fees that vary by area and scope. The outdoor riverside terrace and the SookSiam floor are the most frequently requested filming locations. Allow 1-2 weeks for approval.

**Best time to film:** The indoor spaces are climate-controlled and lit consistently, so timing is flexible. For the riverside terrace and exterior shots, late afternoon provides the best light against the Bangkok skyline across the river. The multimedia fountain show runs in the evening and offers spectacular B-roll opportunities.

**Insider tip:** The Gold Line monorail that connects ICONSIAM to the BTS system provides a unique elevated tracking shot opportunity. The SookSiam floor on the ground level recreates a traditional Thai floating market indoors and offers a cultural element that is far easier to film than an actual floating market.

## 12. Bang Krachao (Bangkok's Green Lung)

**Best for:** Sustainability-focused content, eco-brand storytelling, unique establishing shots, [drone and aerial video](/services/drone-aerial-video/)

Bang Krachao is a vast green peninsula in a bend of the Chao Phraya River, directly across from central Bangkok. Known as Bangkok's "green lung," this area of elevated cycling paths, mangrove forests, small farms, and traditional communities offers a completely different visual narrative from the rest of the city. It is ideal for brands with environmental or wellness positioning.

**Permit requirements:** Much of Bang Krachao is managed by local communities and the Phra Pradaeng district office. Filming permits are less formalized than in central Bangkok but should still be arranged in advance through the district office. Drone permits are more achievable here than in central Bangkok due to distance from airport flight paths, though CAAT registration is still required.

**Best time to film:** Morning (7:00-10:00 AM) when the light filters through the tree canopy and the cycling paths are active but not crowded. Avoid the midday heat, which is intense in the open areas without shade.

**Insider tip:** Hire bicycles for the crew and use them as a mobile filming platform along the elevated paths. The Sri Nakhon Khuean Khan Park at the center of Bang Krachao has a peaceful lake and botanical garden that most visitors miss. Cross the river by the Klong Toei ferry for an atmospheric opening sequence showing the journey from urban Bangkok to green Bang Krachao.

## Bonus: Chatuchak Market and Asiatique

**Chatuchak Weekend Market** is the world's largest outdoor market with over 15,000 stalls. It works well for dynamic B-roll, lifestyle content, and any brand story that benefits from color, energy, and a sense of discovery. Film on Saturday or Sunday mornings (8:00-10:00 AM) before the crowds become unmanageable. The indoor sections of the newer Chatuchak 2 area offer more controlled environments.

**Asiatique The Riverfront** is an open-air mall and entertainment complex on the river built around a former international trading port. The warehouse-style architecture, vintage Ferris wheel, and riverside boardwalk create a casual, accessible atmosphere that suits consumer brand content and [event videography](/services/event-videography/). Filming permits are handled through the Asiatique management office with relatively quick turnaround.

## Practical Filming Tips for Bangkok

### Weather Planning

Bangkok's tropical climate is the single biggest variable in any outdoor production schedule. The cool season (November to February) is the most reliable window for outdoor filming, with temperatures around 25-32 degrees Celsius and low humidity. The hot season (March to May) pushes temperatures above 35 degrees and creates harsh overhead light between 10:00 AM and 3:00 PM. The rainy season (June to October) brings dramatic clouds and afternoon storms, but mornings are usually clear. Always have an indoor backup location scheduled during the rainy season.

### Traffic and Transport Logistics

Bangkok traffic is legendary for a reason. When planning a multi-location shoot day, never schedule a vehicle move during morning rush (7:00-9:00 AM) or evening rush (5:00-8:00 PM). Use the BTS Skytrain, MRT subway, or river boats for crew movement when possible. Group your locations geographically: shoot all riverside locations in one session and all Sukhumvit locations in another. A production vehicle with a knowledgeable driver is essential for equipment transport, even if crew members travel by rail.

### Permit Strategy

The permit landscape in Bangkok involves multiple authorities depending on the location type. The Bangkok Metropolitan Administration (BMA) handles public spaces, the Fine Arts Department oversees temples and historic sites, the CAAT manages drone approvals, and private venues handle their own permissions. Working with a local production company that has established relationships with these authorities saves significant time and reduces the risk of day-of-shoot complications. Most permits can be processed in 3-7 business days, but allow two weeks during holiday periods.

### Gear Considerations

Bangkok's heat and humidity affect equipment. Keep cameras and lenses in air-conditioned vehicles or pelican cases with silica gel packets when not in use. Allow 15-20 minutes for lenses to acclimatize when moving from air-conditioned interiors to outdoor humidity, or you will get condensation fogging. Power is generally reliable, but bring portable battery solutions for park and street locations where mains power is not available.

### Cultural Sensitivity

When filming at temples, all crew members must dress respectfully: covered shoulders and knees, no hats inside temple buildings, and shoes removed where required. Always ask permission before filming monks or religious ceremonies. In markets and street locations, maintain awareness of the space you are occupying and the impact on local vendors and residents. A polite, respectful crew builds goodwill and often leads to better access and cooperation from locals.
